# P562 — Phasing — Not a Zone Change, Counters/Auras Persist, Tokens Survive

## Abstract

**Phasing causes a permanent to be treated as though it doesn't exist without actually leaving the battlefield.** This is the critical distinction: phasing is NOT a zone change. The permanent stays on the battlefield with a "phased out" status. Because it never leaves, no LTB or ETB triggers fire, counters remain, Auras and Equipment stay attached (phasing out indirectly with the permanent), and tokens survive (they don't cease to exist). Phased-out permanents can't be affected by anything — they're invisible to the game. Permanents with the phasing keyword phase out and in automatically during their controller's untap step. Effects can also cause phasing directly (Teferi's Protection). "For as long as" effects lose track of phased-out permanents and may expire. Phased-out permanents phase in before untapping.

## The Definitive Rules

**CR 702.26a (Phasing Keyword):** *"During each player's untap step, before the active player untaps permanents, all phased-in permanents with phasing that player controls 'phase out.' Simultaneously, all phased-out permanents that had phased out under that player's control 'phase in.'"*

**CR 702.26b (Phased Out):** *"If a permanent phases out, its status changes to 'phased out.' Except for rules and effects that specifically mention phased-out permanents, a phased-out permanent is treated as though it does not exist. It can't affect or be affected by anything else in the game."*

**CR 702.26d (NOT a Zone Change):** *"The phasing event doesn't actually cause a permanent to change zones or control, even though it's treated as though it's not on the battlefield and not under its controller's control while it's phased out. Zone-change triggers don't trigger when a permanent phases in or out. Tokens continue to exist on the battlefield while phased out. Counters and stickers remain on a permanent while it's phased out."*

**CR 702.26e (Continuous Effects):** *"If a continuous effect generated by the resolution of a spell or ability modifies the characteristics or changes the controller of any objects, a phased-out permanent won't be included in the set of affected objects."*

**CR 702.26f (Expiring Effects):** *"Continuous effects that affect a phased-out permanent may expire while that permanent is phased out. If so, they will no longer affect that permanent once it's phased in. In particular, effects with 'for as long as' durations that track that permanent end when that permanent phases out because they can no longer see it."*

**CR 702.26g (Indirect Phasing):** *"When a permanent phases out, any Auras, Equipment, or Fortifications attached to that permanent phase out at the same time. This alternate way of phasing out is known as phasing out 'indirectly.'"*

**CR 702.26j (Attach/Unattach Triggers):** *"Abilities that trigger when a permanent becomes attached or unattached from an object or player don't trigger when that permanent phases in or out."*

**Official Ruling (Teferi's Protection, 2017-08-25):** *"Phasing out doesn't cause any 'leaves the battlefield' abilities to trigger. Similarly, phasing in won't cause any 'enters the battlefield' abilities to trigger."*

**Official Ruling (2017-08-25):** *"If a token is phased out, it will phase in as your next untap step begins."*

**Official Ruling (2017-08-25):** *"Permanents that phase out with counters phase in with those counters."*

**Official Ruling (2017-08-25):** *"Choices made for permanents as they entered the battlefield are remembered when they phase in."*

**Official Ruling (2017-08-25):** *"Any one-shot effects that are waiting 'until [this] leaves the battlefield,' such as that of Banishing Light, won't happen when a permanent phases out."*

## The Pattern

```
PHASING VS ZONE CHANGE (CRITICAL):

  Phasing:                           Zone Change (exile/bounce):
  ─────────────────────────────────  ─────────────────────────────
  NOT a zone change                  IS a zone change
  No LTB triggers                    LTB triggers fire
  No ETB triggers on phase-in        ETB triggers fire on return
  Counters preserved                 Counters removed
  Auras stay attached                Auras go to graveyard
  Equipment stays attached           Equipment detaches
  Tokens survive                     Tokens cease to exist
  Choices remembered                 Choices must be made again
  "Until leaves" effects DON'T end   "Until leaves" effects DO end
  Same object throughout             New object on return

PHASING KEYWORD (AUTOMATIC):

  Permanents with "phasing" automatically:
    1. Phase OUT during controller's untap step (before untapping)
    2. Phase IN during controller's NEXT untap step (before untapping)
    3. Repeat each turn

  Timeline per untap step:
    a. Phased-in permanents with phasing → phase out
    b. Phased-out permanents → phase in
    c. Then untap permanents
    (a and b happen simultaneously)

  If untap step is SKIPPED (e.g., Stasis):
    → Phasing events don't occur that turn (702.26m)
    → Permanents stay phased out until next actual untap step

EFFECT-CAUSED PHASING:

  Some effects phase permanents out directly (e.g., Teferi's Protection):
    - "All permanents you control phase out"
    - These phase in during the controller's next untap step
    - Same rules apply: no zone change, counters persist, etc.

PHASED-OUT STATE:

  While phased out, a permanent:
    - Is treated as though it does NOT EXIST
    - Can't be targeted by spells or abilities
    - Static abilities have no effect on the game
    - Triggered abilities can't trigger
    - Can't attack or block
    - Doesn't count for "permanents you control" or similar
    - Is NOT on the battlefield for game purposes (though technically it is)
    - Can't be affected by "destroy all" or similar effects

  What IS preserved:
    - Counters (+1/+1, loyalty, poison, etc.)
    - Stickers
    - Attached Auras (phased out indirectly)
    - Attached Equipment (phased out indirectly)
    - "Enters with" choices (color chosen for Painter's Servant, etc.)
    - Control (controller doesn't change)
    - Zone (still on the battlefield)

AURAS AND EQUIPMENT — INDIRECT PHASING:

  When a permanent phases out (702.26g):
    - All attached Auras phase out WITH it (indirectly)
    - All attached Equipment phase out WITH it (indirectly)
    - Indirectly phased-out attachments phase in WITH the permanent

  Direct vs Indirect phasing:
    - DIRECT: A spell/ability phases out the Aura/Equipment itself
      → Phases in attached to what it was attached to (if still valid)
      → If not valid: phases in unattached (Auras go to GY via SBA)
    - INDIRECT: Aura/Equipment phases out because its host phased out
      → Always phases in still attached to its host

  Attach/unattach triggers DON'T fire (702.26j):
    - "Whenever an Equipment becomes attached" → doesn't trigger
    - "Whenever an Aura becomes unattached" → doesn't trigger

TOKENS AND PHASING:

  Tokens that phase out:
    - Continue to exist on the battlefield (702.26d)
    - This is different from exile: tokens cease to exist when exiled
    - Phase in normally during the next untap step
    - All characteristics preserved

CONTINUOUS EFFECTS AND PHASING:

  Effects FROM spells/abilities (702.26e):
    - Phased-out permanents are excluded from affected objects
    - "+1/+1 to all creatures you control" → doesn't affect phased-out
    - When it phases in: continuous effects recalculate (include it again)

  "For as long as" durations (702.26f):
    - These effects LOSE TRACK of phased-out permanents
    - The effect may expire while the permanent is phased out
    - Example: "Exile target creature for as long as I control [this]"
      → [this] phases out → effect can't see [this] → exile ends
      → The creature returns from exile!

  "Until end of turn" effects:
    - May expire while the permanent is phased out
    - If so, the effect no longer applies when it phases in

  "Until [this] leaves the battlefield" effects (one-shot):
    - DO NOT trigger when the permanent phases out
    - Phasing is NOT leaving the battlefield
    - The exile effect continues; the creature stays exiled

BANISHING LIGHT INTERACTION (IMPORTANT):

  Banishing Light: "Exile target... until Banishing Light leaves"
    - If Banishing Light PHASES OUT: it hasn't left the battlefield
    - The exiled card stays in exile
    - When Banishing Light phases in: nothing changes (it never left)

  Oblivion Ring (separate ETB/LTB triggers, NOT "for as long as"):
    - Has separate "When enters, exile" and "When leaves, return" triggers
    - If O-Ring phases out: LTB doesn't fire (phasing isn't leaving)
    - The exiled card stays exiled (same result as Banishing Light)
    - Both old and new templates keep cards exiled through phasing

PHASING AND COMBAT:

  If a creature phases out during combat (702.26b):
    - It's removed from combat
    - Any damage assignment is lost
    - "Whenever this creature attacks" → already triggered (still on stack)

  Creatures that phase in during the untap step:
    - Are untapped (if they were tapped, they untap normally)
    - CAN attack that turn (they didn't just enter the battlefield)
    - CAN tap for abilities (no summoning sickness issues if they
      had been under your control since your last turn started)
```

## Definitive Conclusions

- **Phasing is NOT a zone change** — no LTB, no ETB, no new object. The permanent never leaves.
- **Counters, Auras, Equipment persist** — everything stays attached through phasing.
- **Tokens survive phasing** — unlike exile, tokens don't cease to exist when phased out.
- **"For as long as" effects may expire** — they lose track of phased-out permanents.
- **"Until leaves" effects DON'T trigger** — Banishing Light's exile stays in effect.
- **Phased-out = doesn't exist** — can't be targeted, doesn't count, abilities inactive.
- **Phase in before untap** — phased-in creatures can attack and tap immediately.

## Canonical Example

**Teferi's Protection:**

You control 5 permanents (including two creatures with +1/+1 counters, one equipped with Lightning Greaves, and one enchanted by an Aura). You cast Teferi's Protection.

All your permanents phase out. The creatures keep their +1/+1 counters. Lightning Greaves phases out indirectly (attached to its creature). The Aura phases out indirectly (attached to its permanent). No LTB triggers fire. Tokens among your permanents survive.

Opponent casts Wrath of God. Your phased-out permanents are not affected (they don't exist).

On your next untap step: all your permanents phase in with counters, Auras, Equipment exactly as they were. No ETB triggers fire. Your creatures can attack this turn (they didn't just enter).

**Example 2 — "For as long as" vs "Until leaves":**

You control Banishing Light (new template: "until Banishing Light leaves the battlefield") exiling an opponent's creature. Banishing Light phases out.

The exiled creature stays in exile. Banishing Light hasn't left the battlefield (phasing isn't leaving). When Banishing Light phases in, nothing changes — the creature remains exiled.

Oblivion Ring uses separate ETB/LTB triggers (not "for as long as" wording). If Oblivion Ring phases out, its LTB trigger doesn't fire — phasing isn't leaving the battlefield. The exiled creature stays exiled, same result as Banishing Light. True "for as long as" cards (like Sower of Temptation: "gain control for as long as you control Sower") may lose track of their effect when the source phases out.

**Example 3 — Token Phasing:**

You control a 3/3 Beast token with two +1/+1 counters (effectively 5/5). An effect phases it out.

The token continues to exist while phased out. It doesn't cease to exist (unlike exile). On your next untap step, the Beast token phases in as a 5/5 (3/3 base + two +1/+1 counters preserved). It can attack immediately.

## Commonly Confused With

- **P003 (Zone Change Identity)** — P003 covers zone changes creating new objects; P562 covers phasing which is explicitly NOT a zone change (same object throughout).
- **P011 (Linked Ability Zone Reset)** — P011 covers linked exile-return pairs; P562 covers phasing where no exile/return occurs.
- **P561 (Protection)** — P561 covers protection from everything (DEBT); Teferi's Protection combines both protection and phasing but they are independent effects.
