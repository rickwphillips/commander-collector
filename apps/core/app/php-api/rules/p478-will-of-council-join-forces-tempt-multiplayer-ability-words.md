# P478 — Will of the Council, Join Forces, and Tempt with — Vote-by-Player, Shared-Mana-Contribution, and Repeating-Reward-Offer Multiplayer Words

## Abstract

Three ability words from Commander and Conspiracy products designed for multiplayer (3+ player) games: **Will of the Council** (Conspiracy, CR 207.2c) has all players vote on an option, with the option receiving the most votes winning; **Join Forces** (Commander 2011, CR 207.2c) allows each player to pay any amount of mana when the ability resolves, with effects scaling by total contribution; and **Tempt with [X]** (Commander 2013, CR 207.2c) offers each opponent a copy of the same benefit, but you get an extra copy for each opponent who accepts. All three create unusual politics, incentives, and group dynamics.

## The Definitive Rules

### Will of the Council (CR 207.2c)
Will of the Council text pattern: *"Will of the Council — Starting with you, each player votes for [Option A] or [Option B]. [Result based on which option received more votes. Ties are resolved as described.]"*

Each player votes once. The option with the most votes wins (ties are typically resolved by the spell's text — e.g., "if votes are tied, [outcome]"). Voting is public (players announce their vote in turn order, so each player knows the prior votes before choosing).

### Join Forces (CR 207.2c)
Join Forces text pattern: *"Join Forces — Starting with you, each player may pay any amount of mana. [Effect scales based on total mana paid.]"*

Each player can contribute any amount of mana. The effect happens for the controller of the Join Forces spell/ability, scaled by the total mana all players contributed. Players aren't required to contribute; they choose their contribution amount.

### Tempt with [X] (CR 207.2c)
Tempt with text pattern: *"Tempt with [X] — Each opponent may [take X]. For each opponent who does, you also [take X]."*

Tempt with offers each opponent a benefit. Any opponent can take the benefit. For each opponent who accepts, the controller of the Tempt-with spell ALSO gets the benefit. The incentive: the controller benefits more when opponents accept.

## The Pattern

```
WILL OF THE COUNCIL pattern:
Voting sequence: "Starting with you, each player votes for [A] or [B]"
  → In turn order: you vote first, then each other player in turn order
  → Votes are public (announced out loud)
  → Strategic timing: voting last = information advantage (you know all prior votes)
  → The spell controller votes FIRST: no information advantage, but sets the tone
  → Tallying: the option with strictly MORE votes wins
  → Ties: specific to each card's text — often "in the event of a tie, [compromise]"
    → E.g., "if tied, both effects happen" or "if tied, the option you chose wins"
  → In a 4-player game with 1 A vote vs. 3 B votes: B wins easily
  → Politics: opponents can vote together to prevent your desired outcome
  → 2-player game (1v1): vote is often just you vs. opponent → 50/50 chance each
    (unless the spell says "you break ties")

JOIN FORCES pattern:
"Starting with you, each player may pay any amount of mana"
  → In turn order: you choose first, each player pays what they want (including 0)
  → Any mana type works (usually): WUBRG or generic, all added to the total
  → Effect scales: usually "[effect] X times total" where X = total mana paid
  → Players paying mana is OPTIONAL — they might contribute to:
    → Help the controller (if the effect benefits everyone)
    → Get their own political benefit
  → Opponents don't HAVE to pay — they can sandbag and not contribute
  → You can pay as much as you want too — you could pay the whole cost yourself
  → In competitive contexts: rarely used (1v1 opponent won't fund your spells)
  → In Commander/group games: opponents may contribute to fund effects that help them

TEMPT WITH pattern:
"Each opponent may [take X benefit]"
  → Offer the benefit to each opponent individually (in turn order)
  → Each opponent independently decides to accept or decline
  → For each accepting opponent: ALSO give the benefit to the controller
  → Result: Controller gets 0–3 extra benefits (one per accepting opponent)
    (plus the base single benefit if any is inherent in the spell)
  → Incentive structure: if your opponents take the benefit, you get MORE benefit
    → This creates a paradox: do opponents take the benefit (giving you more) or decline?
    → If the benefit is very strong, opponents want it and accept → you benefit more
    → If opponents are suspicious, they decline → you get only the base benefit
  → In a 4-player game: controller offers all 3 opponents the same benefit
    → 0 accept: controller gets 0 bonus (only base)
    → 3 accept: controller gets 3 extra copies of the benefit
  → Key: Tempt with [searching a basic land] is common — each opponent gets a free land,
    but so does the controller for each opponent who takes
```

## Definitive Conclusions

**Will of the Council:**
- Will of the Council creates intense political dynamics in multiplayer. You need to convince other players to vote your way. Being the last voter gives information advantage (you know the vote count before choosing).
- In a 4-player game, 3 players can always outvote 1. Will of the Council cards give opponents the ability to collectively override the spell controller's preference.
- Some Will of the Council cards give the controller a tiebreaker option: "if the votes are tied, the votes are broken by [controller]." This is very powerful — effectively guarantees your preferred outcome.
- Key card: *Council's Judgment* (CNS): "Will of the Council — Starting with you, each player votes for a nonland permanent you don't control. Exile each permanent with the most votes or tied for most." Can force removal of opponents' key permanents via group vote.
- Key card: *Tyrant's Choice* (CNS): "Will of the Council — Starting with you, each player votes for death or torture. If death gets more votes, each opponent sacrifices a creature of their choice. If torture gets more votes or the vote is tied, each opponent loses 4 life." Opponents vote between two bad options — the majority loses a creature or life.

**Join Forces:**
- Join Forces was designed for "group hug" or "force-multiplier" Commander cards. In practice, opponents rarely fund your spells unless the effect directly benefits them too.
- Join Forces with an effect that benefits everyone (like gaining life for all) is more likely to see contributions. An effect that only benefits the controller (like drawing cards) rarely gets opponent funding.
- Key card: *Collective Voyage* (CMD11): "Join Forces — Starting with you, each player may pay any amount of mana. Each player who paid mana searches their library for up to X basic land cards, where X is the total amount of mana paid." All players who contributed get to search for basics — mutual acceleration for group buy-in.
- Key card: *Minds Aglow* (CMD11): "Join Forces — Starting with you, each player may pay any amount of mana. Each player draws X cards, where X is the total amount of mana paid by all players." Pure card draw for everyone who contributes.

**Tempt with:**
- Tempt with creates the "prisoner's dilemma" of Commander politics: each opponent must decide without knowing what others will do (in simultaneous choice) — or if announced in turn order, later opponents know how many accepted.
- Common pattern: Tempt with [basic land search]. Opponents almost always accept — free land drops are extremely valuable. Controller gets 1–3 free lands depending on how many accept. Net result: game state accelerates for everyone.
- Key card: *Tempt with Discovery* (CMD13): "Tempting offer — Search your library for a land card and put it onto the battlefield. Each opponent may search their library for a land card and put it onto the battlefield. For each opponent who searches, you may search your library for a land card and put it onto the battlefield." All lands (including nonbasic); the controller gets one land unconditionally plus one more per accepting opponent. Very powerful in Command Zone strategies.
- Key card: *Tempt with Vengeance* (CMD13): "Tempt with Vengeance — Each opponent may have you create X 1/1 red Elemental creature tokens. For each opponent who does, create X additional 1/1 red Elemental tokens." Opponents get X tokens; you get X additional tokens per accepting opponent. A token army generator with political buy-in.

## Canonical Examples

**Will of the Council:**
- *Council's Judgment*: 4-player game, controller targets opponent's Blightsteel Colossus (which can't be targeted by most effects). Vote for non-land permanents — 3 players can collectively vote for Blightsteel and exile it. No protection from targeting needed.

**Join Forces:**
- *Collective Voyage*: Everyone pays {2}, total = {8}. Each contributing player searches for 8 basics. Accelerates everyone's mana by many turns.

**Tempt with:**
- *Tempt with Discovery*: Controller searches for 1 land unconditionally. All 3 opponents accept (each finding a land). Controller searches for 3 more lands (1 per accepting opponent), totaling 4 lands found. In a high-power Commander game, this fetches Cabal Coffers, Urborg, Nykthos, etc.

## Commonly Confused With

- **P467** (Hidden Agenda/Parley) — Parley is another group-reveal mechanic from Conspiracy; both Parley and Will of the Council involve all players doing something simultaneously or in order; different mechanics but same political design space
- **P451** (Eminence) — Eminence works from the command zone automatically; Will of the Council triggers on resolution; both are Commander-adjacent but completely different mechanisms
- **P460** (Suspect/Forage) — These are individual keyword actions; Will of the Council / Join Forces / Tempt with are multiplayer group-decision mechanics; nothing in common mechanically
- **P445** (Companion) — Companion and Tempt with are both from "outside normal casting" in some sense? No — nothing in common. These are completely unrelated mechanics.
