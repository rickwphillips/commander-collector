# P234 — Shadow and Horsemanship — Niche Evasion Abilities

## Abstract
Two highly restrictive evasion abilities that only exist in limited card sets. **Shadow**: creatures with shadow can only be blocked by creatures WITH shadow, and creatures without shadow cannot be blocked by shadow creatures — effectively creating two separate "planes" of combat. **Horsemanship**: from Portal: Three Kingdoms, functions identically to Flying but only interacts with other horsemanship creatures — "flying for Portal." Both are so restrictive that playing them in a deck typically means they're unblockable unless opponents run the specific ability type. Shadow was in the Tempest block and is the more well-known of the two.

## The Definitive Rules

**CR 702.28a** (verbatim): *"Shadow is an evasion ability."*

**CR 702.28b** (verbatim): *"A creature with shadow can't be blocked by creatures without shadow, and a creature without shadow can't be blocked by creatures with shadow. (See rule 509, 'Declare Blockers Step.')"*

**CR 702.28c** (verbatim): *"Multiple instances of shadow on the same creature are redundant."*

**CR 702.31a** (verbatim): *"Horsemanship is an evasion ability."*

**CR 702.31b** (verbatim): *"A creature with horsemanship can't be blocked by creatures without horsemanship. A creature with horsemanship can block a creature with or without horsemanship. (See rule 509, 'Declare Blockers Step.')"*

**CR 702.31c** (verbatim): *"Multiple instances of horsemanship on the same creature are redundant."*

## The Pattern

```
SHADOW:
  Evasion ability: creates a "dimension split" in combat
  Shadow creature vs. normal creatures: NEITHER can block the other
  Shadow creature vs. shadow creature: CAN block (normal combat between shadow creatures)

  SHADOW ASYMMETRY:
    Your shadow creature attacks opponent's normal creatures → unblockable
    Your shadow creature attacks opponent's shadow creature → normal block rules
    Opponent's shadow creature attacks you → your normal creatures can't block it
    Unless you have shadow creatures: all opposing shadow creatures are unblockable TO YOU

  SHADOW + REACH:
    Reach allows blocking flying creatures. Does Reach help block Shadow?
    No — Reach is specifically about Flying creatures (see rule 702.17)
    Shadow is not the same as Flying
    Shadow creatures can't be blocked by non-shadow creatures regardless of Reach

  SHADOW CARDS (Tempest Block):
    Soltari Monk ({1}{W}{W}): 2/1 Protection from red, Shadow — classic white shadow beater
    Dauthi Slayer ({B}{B}): 2/2 Shadow — attacks each turn if able (black shadow aggro)
    Dauthi Voidwalker ({B}{B}): 3/2 Shadow, modern card: exile GY + flash cast from exile on death
    Soltari Visionary ({1}{W}): 2/1 Shadow, "destroy enchantment blocking it" — combat trick
    Shadow cards were the "unblockable" engine in Tempest Block

  SHADOW MIRROR MATCH:
    Both players running shadow decks: all shadow creatures can block each other normally
    The entire "unblockable" advantage disappears!
    In Tempest block draft: rare to face mirrors → shadow was dominant

HORSEMANSHIP:
  Works LIKE Flying but is a separate keyword
  Horsemanship creature: CAN'T be blocked by creatures WITHOUT horsemanship
  Horsemanship creature: CAN block creatures with OR without horsemanship
  Critical asymmetry: horsemanship creatures can block non-horsemanship creatures!
    Flying: flying can only block flying or reach
    Horsemanship: can block anything, but can only BE blocked by horsemanship

  HORSEMANSHIP + FLYING:
    Flying creature vs. horsemanship creature: neither has the other's ability
    Can a flying creature block a horsemanship creature? NO — horsemanship requires horsemanship to block it
    Can a horsemanship creature block a flying creature? It depends on reach or flying:
    Horsemanship alone can't block flyers (would need flying or reach)
    So horsemanship and flying are SEPARATE dimensions of evasion

  HORSEMANSHIP CARDS (Portal: Three Kingdoms):
    Riding Red Hare ({2}{W}): 3/3 Horsemanship — benchmark Portal uncommon
    Lu Bu, Master-at-Arms ({2}{B}{B}): 4/3 horsemanship, first strike, haste
    Cao Cao, Lord of Wei ({3}{B}{B}): 3/3 horsemanship, tap: opponent discards 2 cards
    Guan Yu, Sainted Warrior ({4}{W}{W}): 4/4 horsemanship, "if Guan Yu would go to GY, put it on top of library"
    Portal: Three Kingdoms has numerous legendary horsemanship creatures with powerful abilities

SHADOW vs HORSEMANSHIP:
  Shadow: MUTUAL restriction — shadow creatures can only attack/block other shadow creatures
  Horsemanship: ONE-WAY restriction — horsemanship can't be blocked by non-horsemanship, but CAN block non-horsemanship
  Horsemanship is offensive AND defensive; Shadow only creates a separate dimension
```

## Definitive Conclusions

- **Shadow creatures are in a separate combat "plane"** — shadow vs non-shadow means neither can block the other.
- **Horsemanship is asymmetric** — unblockable except to other horsemanship, but can block anything.
- **Flying does NOT help** block shadow or horsemanship — they're separate ability types.
- **Shadow mirror matches** eliminate the evasion advantage entirely.
- **Horsemanship creatures can block non-horsemanship attackers** — offensive unblockability with defensive flexibility.

## Canonical Example
**Dauthi Slayer in Tempest Block Aggro:**
Dauthi Slayer ({B}{B}): 2/2 Shadow — "Dauthi Slayer attacks each turn if able."
In a Tempest block deck with Dauthi Slayer + Soltari Monk: pure unblockable aggression.
Opponent controls 10 creatures — none with shadow: Dauthi Slayer attacks every turn unblocked.
2 damage per turn: 5 turns to finish opponent if no other damage.
Combined with equipment or pump spells: faster clock.
Dauthi Voidwalker (modern reprint): 3/2 Shadow for {B}{B} — 3 damage per turn unblocked.

**Example 2 — Cao Cao, Lord of Wei (Horsemanship):**
Cao Cao ({3}{B}{B}): 3/3 Horsemanship — "Tap: Target opponent discards 2 cards."
Opponent controls Baneslayer Angel (5/5 flying): can Baneslayer block Cao Cao?
No — horsemanship requires horsemanship to block; flying is irrelevant.
Cao Cao attacks: 3 combat damage + tap ability = 2 discards per turn.
Opponent has a 1/1 soldier: can Cao Cao block it if opponent attacks?
Yes! Horsemanship creatures CAN block non-horsemanship.
So Cao Cao is both unblockable attacker AND available as a blocker.
This asymmetry makes horsemanship more defensively flexible than Shadow.

## Commonly Confused With
- **P201 (Protection)** — Protection from [color] prevents damage/blocking from that color. Shadow and Horsemanship are evasion abilities based on ability types, not colors.
- **P202 (Trample/Deathtouch)** — Trample assigns excess damage to players; these are combat assignment mechanics. Shadow/Horsemanship affect whether blocking is possible at all.
- **P173 (Exalted)** — Exalted rewards solo attacking. Shadow/Horsemanship are always-on evasion regardless of attack composition.
