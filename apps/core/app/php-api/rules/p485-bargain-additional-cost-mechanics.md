# P487 — Bargain — Sacrifice as Additional Cost

## Abstract

Bargain is an optional additional cost mechanic from Wilds of Eldraine (WOE, 2023). When casting a Bargain spell, you may sacrifice exactly one artifact, enchantment, or token as an additional cost. If you do, the spell is "bargained," which unlocks bonus effects. Because bargain is paid as an additional cost during casting — before the spell is on the stack — the sacrifice happens whether or not the spell ultimately resolves. Tokens are explicitly valid bargain payment, including tokens created in the same turn, or even tokens generated by the spell's own ETB setup on a prior cast. Copies of a bargained spell inherit the bargained status, but permanent tokens entering as copies do not.

## The Definitive Rule

**CR 601.2b / 601.2d**: Additional costs are declared and paid as part of the casting process. Sacrificing a permanent as a cost removes it from the battlefield before the spell resolves or can be countered.

**CR 700.3**: A spell is "bargained" if its bargain cost was paid. This is a property the spell carries on the stack.

**CR 118.6**: Paying a cost is not an effect — it cannot be responded to separately from the spell itself. The sacrifice and the spell are declared simultaneously.

**CR 706.10 / 116.3**: Copies of spells on the stack copy properties of the source, including whether it was bargained. Creating a permanent as a copy of an already-on-battlefield permanent does NOT copy the bargained flag, since that flag is a spell-on-stack property only.

**Official Rulings (2023-09-01)**:
- "Bargain means 'As an additional cost to cast this spell, you may sacrifice an artifact, enchantment, or token.'"
- "You may sacrifice only one artifact, enchantment, or token to pay a spell's bargain cost."
- "If you copy a bargained spell, the copy is also bargained."
- "If a card or token enters the battlefield as a copy of a permanent that's already on the battlefield, the new permanent isn't bargained, even if the original was."

## The Pattern

```
BARGAIN PAYMENT TIMING:
  Bargain cost is paid during casting (CR 601.2b/601.2d).
  Sacrifice occurs before the spell is on the stack.
  The sacrifice cannot be responded to in isolation — it happens as part of casting.

  Player announces "I cast Beseech the Mirror with bargain,
    sacrificing [artifact/enchantment/token]"
    → Sacrifice is paid as cost
    → Spell is on the stack and is BARGAINED
    → Opponents may respond to the spell (counter it, etc.)
    → If countered: spell is gone, sacrifice is still paid (PERMANENT IS STILL DEAD)
    → If it resolves: bargained bonus applies

TOKEN BARGAIN PAYMENT:
  You can sacrifice a token to pay bargain.
  The token may have been created at any prior point — even this same turn.
  Example: Create a Food token (The Witch's Vanity, Chapter II), then bargain
    with Chapter III of the same Saga → food is a valid bargain payment.
  Example: Charming Scoundrel creates a Treasure token (mode 2).
    That Treasure can be sacrificed to bargain a later spell this same turn.

DOES BARGAIN COUNT AS "SACRIFICING AN ENCHANTMENT"?
  Yes. If you sacrifice a Role token (which is an enchantment token)
    to pay bargain, this is a sacrifice of an enchantment AND a sacrifice of a token.
  Effects that trigger on "whenever you sacrifice an enchantment" (e.g., Starfield Mystic,
    Auratog, Enchantress effects that care about enchantments leaving) WILL trigger.
  Effects that trigger on "whenever you sacrifice a token" also trigger.
  Bargain does not suppress triggers — it is just the mechanism of sacrifice.

COPY OF BARGAINED SPELL:
  If you copy a Bargain spell after it's on the stack (e.g., Strionic Resonator on ETB,
    or Reverberate, Fork, Twincast on an instant/sorcery) — the copy is also bargained.
  You do NOT pay any additional cost for the copy — copies are never cast.
  The copy resolves with the bargained bonus without you sacrificing anything extra.

BESEECH THE MIRROR FREE-CAST WINDOW:
  Beseech the Mirror searches for a card, exiles face-down, THEN you may cast it free
    if it's bargained and MV ≤ 4.
  This cast happens during resolution of Beseech.
  This is a mid-resolution cast — similar to cascade or suspend.
  You may choose not to cast it; it goes to hand.
  If you cast a spell with "as an additional cost to cast" requirements (e.g., a card
    that requires sacrificing a permanent), you MUST pay those costs.
    Beseech only waives the mana cost, not other additional costs.

COST REDUCTION BARGAIN (Hamlet Glutton):
  "This spell costs {2} less to cast if it's bargained."
  This is a cost reduction, not a triggered bonus.
  It is evaluated during the total cost calculation step (CR 601.2f).
  To get the reduction, you must declare the bargain cost AND satisfy it.
  You cannot declare "I will bargain" to get the reduction and then fail to sacrifice.
```

## Definitive Conclusions

- **The sacrifice is permanent if the spell is countered.** You gave up the artifact/enchantment/token; you do not get it back. Bargain is an additional cost, not a trigger — there is no refund.
- **Any token works for bargain payment**, including ones created the same turn, Role tokens (enchantment tokens), Food, Clue, Treasure, and creature tokens. The token type does not restrict bargain unless it lacks the artifact, enchantment, or "token" status (creature tokens that are not also artifacts or enchantments ARE valid because they ARE tokens — the three categories in bargain are OR, not AND).
- **Sacrificing a Role token to bargain triggers enchantment-care effects.** Roles are enchantments (type: Enchantment — Aura Role). Their sacrifice is a sacrifice of an enchantment for all purposes.
- **Copies of bargained spells are bargained for free.** The copied spell inherits the bargained status without requiring a second sacrifice.
- **A permanent entering as a copy of a bargained permanent is NOT bargained.** The "bargained" status is a spell-on-stack property, not a permanent characteristic. Once the spell resolves, the permanent knows nothing about being bargained (unless its own text cares about "if it was bargained" on ETB — those are checked once as the ability triggers, then forgotten).
- **Beseech the Mirror's free-cast is a cast, not a put onto battlefield.** All cast rules apply — you can pay additional costs, kicker, etc. The only thing waived is the mana cost.

## Canonical Examples

**Example 1 — Bargain with a Role Token:**
You control a creature with a Wicked Role token attached. You cast Beseech the Mirror, sacrificing the Wicked Role to bargain. The Wicked Role is an enchantment token — valid bargain payment. Beseech is now bargained. The Wicked Role's "when put into a graveyard" trigger fires (each opponent loses 1 life). Beseech resolves; you may cast a MV ≤ 4 card for free.

**Example 2 — Countered Bargain:**
You cast Torch the Tower (bargain), sacrificing a Treasure token. Opponent counters with Counterspell. Torch the Tower does not resolve. The Treasure is gone regardless — it was sacrificed as a cost before the spell was put on the stack.

**Example 3 — Copy of Beseech the Mirror:**
You cast Beseech the Mirror with bargain (sacrificed a Food token). Opponent controls Isochron Scepter with Fork imprinted? No — suppose you have Strionic Resonator and Beseech has an ETB trigger? Actually: suppose you cast Beseech and an opponent uses Bonus Round (copies all your sorceries). The copy of Beseech is also bargained. The copy may also cast its found card for free, without any additional sacrifice.

**Example 4 — Hamlet Glutton cost reduction:**
You cast Hamlet Glutton `{5}{G}{G}`. You pay bargain by sacrificing a Treasure token. The spell costs {2} less: you pay `{3}{G}{G}` total. The Treasure is gone (cost paid), and the 7/7 trample enters with its life gain trigger.

**Example 5 — Realm-Scorcher Hellkite mana burst:**
You cast Realm-Scorcher Hellkite `{4}{R}{R}` with bargain (sacrifice any enchantment). It enters the battlefield. Because it was bargained, its ETB trigger fires: add four mana in any combination of colors. This mana is added during the resolution of the trigger — it can be used to cast instant-speed spells or activate abilities in response to triggers on the stack, but floats to the next phase if not used. Note: the "if it was bargained" check is checked once as the triggered ability is put on the stack; it is always true if the spell resolved with bargain paid.

## Commonly Confused With

- **P003 (Zone Change Identity)** — The "sacrificed permanent doesn't return if spell is countered" is not a zone-change identity question; it's simply because costs are paid and not reversed.
- **P025 (Counter Placement — Cost vs. Effect)** — That pattern covers Doubling Season and loyalty cost vs. effect. Bargain cost vs. bonus is analogous: the sacrifice is a cost (not doubled by Doubling Season), while the bonus tokens or effects on the spell's ETB could be affected by Doubling Season.
- **P023 (Trigger Suppression vs. Replacement)** — Torpor Orb suppresses "if it was bargained" ETB triggers on permanents (e.g., Realm-Scorcher Hellkite's mana burst won't fire under Torpor Orb). The bargain cost is still paid; you just get no trigger.
