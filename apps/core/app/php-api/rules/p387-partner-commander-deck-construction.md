# P387 — Partner — Commander Deck Construction with Two Commanders

## Abstract
**Partner** (702.124) is a group of keyword abilities that allow a player to designate TWO legendary cards as their commander instead of one. There are multiple distinct partner variants: **partner** (generic — any two legends with partner), **partner with [name]** (only the two named cards), **partner—[text]** (two legends with the same flavor-text-partner tag, like "Friends forever"), **choose a Background** (a legend + a legendary Background enchantment), and **Doctor's companion** (a Time Lord Doctor + a companion creature). Critical rules: the two commanders begin the game together in the command zone; the deck's color identity is the COMBINED color identities of both; commander tax is tracked independently for each; 21 combat damage is tracked independently for each; when an effect refers to "your commander" you choose which applies. Different partner variants cannot be mixed.

## The Definitive Rules

**CR 702.124a** (verbatim): *"Partner abilities are keyword abilities that modify the rules for deck construction in the Commander variant (see rule 903), and they function before the game begins. Each partner ability allows you to designate two legendary cards as your commander rather than one."*

**CR 702.124b** (verbatim): *"Your deck must contain exactly 100 cards, including its two commanders. Both commanders begin the game in the command zone."*

**CR 702.124c** (verbatim): *"A rule or effect that refers to your commander's color identity refers to the combined color identities of your two commanders. See rule 903.4."*

**CR 702.124d** (verbatim): *"Except for determining the color identity of your commander, the two commanders function independently. When casting a commander with partner, ignore how many times your other commander has been cast (see rule 903.8). When determining whether a player has been dealt 21 or more combat damage by the same commander, consider damage from each of your two commanders separately (see rule 903.10a)."*

**CR 702.124e** (verbatim): *"If an effect refers to your commander while you have two commanders, it refers to either one. If an effect causes you to perform an action on your commander and it could affect both, you choose which it refers to at the time the effect is applied."*

**CR 702.124f** (verbatim): *"Different partner abilities are distinct from one another and cannot be combined. For example, you cannot designate two cards as your commander if one of them has 'partner' and the other has 'partner with [name].'"*

**CR 702.124h** (verbatim): *"'Partner' means 'You may designate two legendary cards as your commander rather than one if each of them has partner.'"*

**CR 702.124j** (verbatim): *"'Partner with [name]' represents two abilities. It means 'You may designate two legendary cards as your commander rather than one if each has a "partner with [name]" ability with the other's name' and 'When this permanent enters, target player may search their library for a card named [name], reveal it, put it into their hand, then shuffle.'"*

**CR 702.124k** (verbatim): *"'Choose a Background' means 'You may designate two cards as your commander rather than one if one of them is this card and the other is a legendary Background enchantment card.'"*

## The Pattern

```
PARTNER VARIANTS (702.124a):
  1. PARTNER (generic):
     702.124h: any two legendary creatures/cards that both have "Partner" (the single word).
     Mix and match: any Partner card + any other Partner card.
     Examples: Tymna the Weaver + Thrasios, Triton Hero; Vial Smasher the Fierce + any Partner.
     Most flexible: hundreds of combinations.
  2. PARTNER WITH [NAME]:
     702.124j: specific two-card pairs only.
     "Partner with [name]" on card A names card B; card B has "Partner with [card A's name]."
     Only those two specific cards can partner together.
     Examples: Akiri, Line-Slinger (partner with Bruse Tarl, Boorish Herder) and vice versa.
     Also: when either enters the battlefield, you may search your library for the other and put
       it in hand. Built-in tutoring.
  3. PARTNER—[TEXT] (flavor tags):
     702.124i: two cards with the SAME "partner—[text]" ability.
     "Friends forever" — Battle for Baldur's Gate set: any two "friends forever" cards partner.
     "Character select" — specific set. "Father & son" — specific set.
     "Survivors" — specific set.
     These are like "Partner" (generic) but limited to cards with the same flavor tag.
  4. CHOOSE A BACKGROUND:
     702.124k: one creature with "choose a Background" + one legendary Background enchantment.
     Background enchantments: Enchantment subtype "Background." Only these pair with "choose a Background."
     Example: Commander from CLB with "choose a Background" + any legendary Background like
       "Criminal Past" ({2}{B}: Background, "your commander has 'whenever a creature you control
       deals combat damage to a player, that player loses 1 life.'")
     The Background stays in the command zone alongside the creature commander.
     Both start in the command zone. You may cast either from there.
  5. DOCTOR'S COMPANION:
     702.124m: one Time Lord Doctor legendary creature + a card with "Doctor's companion."
     The Doctor must be a legendary Time Lord Doctor creature with no OTHER creature types.
     Very specific to Doctor Who set.

BOTH COMMANDERS START IN COMMAND ZONE (702.124b):
  Your 100-card deck includes BOTH commanders.
  Both begin in command zone (two commanders there simultaneously).
  You may cast either from the command zone at any time you could cast that spell.
  Both can be on the battlefield at the same time.
  Both are subject to legend rule independently (each is its own legendary permanent).
  If opponent also controls Thrasios: one must be sacrificed (legend rule).

COMBINED COLOR IDENTITY (702.124c):
  Deck construction: colors allowed = UNION of both commanders' color identities.
  Example: Tymna the Weaver ({W}{B}: Orzhov) + Thrasios, Triton Hero ({U}{G}: Simic).
    Combined: {W}{U}{B}{G} = 4-color Yore-Tiller (no red). Any W/U/B/G card is legal.
  Example: Vial Smasher the Fierce ({B}{R}) + Kraum, Ludevic's Opus ({U}{R}).
    Combined: {U}{B}{R} = Grixis. Any U/B/R card is legal.
  You can build a 4-color deck with just 2 partners if they cover different color pairs.
  You CAN build a 5-color deck with two Partners if between them they cover all 5 colors.

INDEPENDENT TRACKING (702.124d):
  COMMANDER TAX:
    Each commander has its own tax (number of times cast from command zone).
    Casting Tymna 3 times: {2} extra on Tymna. Thrasios unaffected.
    Casting Thrasios once: {2} extra on Thrasios. Tymna unaffected.
    The "ignore other commander's cast count" = no shared tax.
  COMMANDER DAMAGE:
    Each commander tracks its own 21 damage rule independently.
    If Tymna deals 21 combat damage to a player: that player loses.
    If Thrasios deals 21 combat damage: also wins independently.
    21 damage from "both commanders combined" does NOT count (702.124d).
    Example: Tymna deals 10, Thrasios deals 11: no one dies from commander damage.
      The player would need 21 from EACH, not combined.

EFFECTS THAT REFER TO "YOUR COMMANDER" (702.124e):
  "Return your commander from the command zone": which one? You choose.
  "Your commander gains trample": which one? You choose which it applies to.
  "If you control your commander": you control the effect if you control EITHER.
  "You may put your commander on top of your library": choose which commander.
  "Whenever you cast your commander": triggers for either one.

COMMANDER TAX AND BOTH IN PLAY:
  If both commanders are on the battlefield: two separate commanders.
  They're both legendary. If a copy-creature tries to be both: legend rule applies separately.
  If an opponent attacks with a "clone of Tymna" into your Tymna: legend rule fires.
    The owner of the real Tymna chooses. If that's you, you choose which to keep.
    Typically: keep the real one (it has commander history). Sacrifice the clone?
    Actually: the legend rule says the OWNER (or controller) of a legendary permanent with a
      duplicate must sacrifice one. If you control both (your Tymna + their clone Tymna):
      You must sacrifice one. Keep yours (it's your commander). Sacrifice the clone.
      But wait: you don't control the clone if opponent controls it.
      Both you and opponent have Tymna in play. Legend rule: EACH player, for each legend they
        control with the same name, must choose one to keep.
      You keep your Tymna. Opponent keeps their Tymna (the clone). Both stay.
      Actually: 704.5j: "If a player controls two or more legendary permanents with the same name,
        that player chooses one to remain on the battlefield, then puts the rest into their owners'
        graveyards." Each player independently resolves this for THEIR permanents.
      You control 1 Tymna: no action needed. Opponent controls 1 clone-Tymna: no action needed.
      Both remain. (Legend rule applies per controller, not per name across all players.)

PARTNER WITH [NAME] TUTORING:
  702.124j: when EITHER partner enters the battlefield, you may search for the other.
  If Akiri, Line-Slinger enters: you may search for Bruse Tarl, Boorish Herder and put it in hand.
    Then you can cast Bruse from hand (normal cast — no commander tax).
  This is a strong synergy: one partner tutors the other for free when it ETBs.
  Effectively: casting either partner searches for the other.

CHOOSE A BACKGROUND — SPECIAL RULES:
  Background stays in command zone (it's your second "commander").
  Background enchantments are typically not creatures — they're enchantments.
  You can cast the Background from the command zone: it ETBs as an enchantment on the battlefield.
  The Background's text says things like "your commander has [ability]" — it grants abilities
    to your creature commander.
  When the Background is on the battlefield and your creature commander is also there:
    The creature commander has the Background's granted abilities.
  If the Background is in the command zone: the granted abilities may NOT apply (depends on wording).
    Most Background texts say "your commander has X" — this might apply from command zone or not.
    Typically: the Background needs to be on the battlefield to grant abilities to your commander.
    Cast the Background to activate its bonus. Then cast your creature commander to get the bonus.
```

## Definitive Conclusions

- **Both commanders begin in the command zone and can both be on the battlefield** — they are tracked independently for tax and 21-damage; the deck's color identity is the union of both.
- **Commander tax is independent** — casting Commander A does not increase Commander B's tax; each commander has its own cast-from-command-zone counter.
- **21 commander damage is per-commander, not combined** — 11 from Commander A + 11 from Commander B = no commander damage loss; a player needs 21 from the SAME commander.
- **Different partner variants cannot be combined** — "Partner" + "partner with [name]" is illegal; "choose a Background" + "Friends forever" is illegal; only same-variant cards can pair.
- **"Your commander" effects refer to either one** — when an effect could apply to both, you choose which one.

## Canonical Example
**Tymna + Thrasios — 4-Color Competitive Commander:**
Commanders: Tymna the Weaver ({1}{W}{B}: 2/2 lifelink, "Pay X life where X = number of opponents dealt combat damage this turn: draw X cards") + Thrasios, Triton Hero ({1}{U}{G}: 1/3, "{4}: scry 1, then reveal top card; if it's a land, put it on battlefield tapped, else put it in hand").

Combined color identity: W + B + U + G = 4 colors. No red. Build a WUBG (Yore-Tiller) deck.

Turn structure (mid-game):
- Tymna is in play. After combat: 3 opponents took combat damage. Pay 3 life → draw 3 cards.
- Tymna has been cast from command zone twice: next cast costs {1}{W}{B} + {4} = {5}{W}{B}.
- Thrasios is in command zone (not yet cast). Cost: {1}{U}{G} (first cast — no tax).
- Cast Thrasios for {1}{U}{G}: enters the battlefield. Activate "{4}" ability each turn.

Commander damage tracking:
- Opponent A has taken 8 commander damage from Tymna over several turns.
  They need 13 more from Tymna to lose.
- Opponent A has taken 0 commander damage from Thrasios (Thrasios rarely attacks — low power).
- If Tymna hits Opponent A 4 more times (2 damage/attack):
  8 + (4 × 2) = 16. Not 21 yet.
  But if Tymna gets pumped: one hit of 13 damage would do it.

Tax scenario:
Tymna dies 3 times. Next cast: {1}{W}{B} + {6} = {7}{W}{B}. Very expensive.
Thrasios dies twice. Next cast: {1}{U}{G} + {4} = {5}{U}{G}. Still manageable.
The taxes are completely independent — no crossover.

**Example 2 — Partner With [Name] — Akiri + Bruse Tarl:**
You cast Akiri, Line-Slinger ({2}{R}{W}: 0/3, first strike, vigilance; "Akiri gets +1/+0 for each artifact you control"; "partner with Bruse Tarl, Boorish Herder").

Akiri ETBs. "Partner with" triggered ability fires: "Target player may search their library for a card named Bruse Tarl, Boorish Herder, reveal it, put it into their hand, then shuffle."
You search: find Bruse Tarl. Put it in your hand.

Next turn: cast Bruse Tarl ({4}{R}{W}: 3/3, "Whenever Bruse Tarl attacks, you and Bruse Tarl each deal 1 damage to up to one target each") from your HAND.
No commander tax for casting from hand.
Bruse Tarl ETBs: triggers "partner with Akiri, Line-Slinger" — you may search for Akiri. But Akiri is already on the battlefield. Still works (search library for Akiri — it's not there). You'd say "decline to search" since Akiri is in play.

Key benefit: casting one partner early tutors the other into hand for free.
You cast Akiri on turn 3 (cheap, 3 mana). Tutor Bruse for free. Cast Bruse from hand on turn 7.
No extra tax on Bruse (hand cast). You've effectively paid the tax only on Akiri's later casts.

Combined color identity: R + W = Boros. Build mono-R/W (aggressive equipment deck).

## Commonly Confused With
- **P378 (Commander Rules)** — Commander tax and 21-damage rules are covered there. Partner extends those rules for two commanders. The key extension: independence (P387) vs. the standard rules (P378). Both patterns should be read together for complete Commander rules.
- **P379 (Multiplayer — Leaving the Game)** — When a player with two commanders leaves the game: both commanders leave the game with all their owned objects (800.4a). The commander-zone designations leave with the player.
- **P003 (Zone Change — New Object)** — When a commander with partner moves to a zone and then back to the command zone via the SBA (903.9a): it's treated as the same commander for tax/damage tracking. Even with two partners, each is independently tracked as the same object for commander purposes.
- **P380 (Voting)** — "Partner with [name]"'s "may search" trigger isn't a vote. It's a triggered ability that fires on ETB. The controller of the trigger chooses whether to search — it's a "may" ability, not a vote.
