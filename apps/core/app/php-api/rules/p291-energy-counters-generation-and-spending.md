# P291 — Counters on Players — Poison, Energy, Tickets, and Experience

## Abstract
Counters can be placed on players, not just permanents. The primary types of player counters are: **Poison counters** (10+ = lose the game), **Energy counters** ({E} symbols — a resource spent for effects), **Ticket counters** (from Unfinity — used with Attractions), and **Experience counters** (from Commander 2016 — grow your commander abilities over the game). Each counter type has different rules — poison counters kill, energy counters are spent as mana-like resources, experience counters scale abilities, and ticket counters trigger certain Attraction effects.

## The Definitive Rules

**CR 122.1** (verbatim): *"A counter is a marker placed on an object or player to modify its characteristics and/or track a dynamic quantity."*

**CR 122.2** (verbatim): *"Adding or moving a counter is a one-shot effect. See rule 611, 'One-Shot Effects.'"*

**SBA 704.5c**: *"If a player has ten or more poison counters, that player loses the game."*

## The Pattern

```
POISON COUNTERS:
  Gained from: Infect (P153/separate), Toxic (P263), specific effects
  10+ poison counters: player loses the game (SBA 704.5c)
  Poison counters don't go away: they persist unless specifically removed
  No normal way to remove poison counters (very rare: Leeches, Vorinclex rare interaction)
  Proliferate (P216): can add poison counters to players

  POISON COUNTER THRESHOLD:
    At exactly 10: you lose immediately at the next SBA check
    Opponents will often hold a final poison counter to "kill" at 10
    No comeback: once at 9, any source of 1 more poison = lose
    Strategic: poison strategies aim for exact 10, often via small toxic creatures + proliferate

  POISON + MULTIPLE SOURCES:
    Multiple instances of Infect/Toxic add together toward 10
    Infect damage = poison counters to the player (not life loss) + -1/-1 counters to creatures
    Toxic: regular damage + N poison counters
    A player at 5 life and 5 poison: can lose to either more damage OR 5 more poison

ENERGY COUNTERS ({E}):
  Created by specific Kaladesh-era cards
  Players hold energy as a resource
  Spent: cards say "Pay {E}" or "Pay {E}{E}" to activate effects
  Energy counters are tracked on the player (not permanents)
  Energy: persistent across turns until spent
  Proliferate can add energy counters to players

  ENERGY GENERATION:
    "You get {E}{E}{E}" — gain 3 energy counters
    Energy can be generated by spells, activated abilities, triggered abilities
    Stockpile energy: hold onto it, spend it when needed

  ENERGY SPENDING:
    Spending is a cost: "Pay {E}": remove 1 energy counter from yourself
    Can only spend energy you currently have
    Energy doesn't "untap" or refresh — it's a spendable resource (like life, but for activations)

  ENERGY NOTABLE CARDS (Kaladesh):
    Aetherworks Marvel ({4}, Artifact): "{T}: Add {E}{E}{E}." Wait: "Whenever a permanent you control
      is put into a graveyard, you get {E}. Pay {E}{E}{E}{E}{E}{E}, {T}: Look at top 6 cards, cast
      one for free."
      Aetherworks Marvel: generate energy by having permanents die, then spin for 6 free.
      In a combo deck: generate 6 energy quickly, cast Ulamog, the Ceaseless Hunger for free from top 6.

    Harnessed Lightning ({1}{R}, Instant): "You get {E}{E}{E}. Then you may pay any amount of {E}.
      Harnessed Lightning deals damage to target creature equal to the amount of {E} paid."
      Versatile: deal 0 damage and store 3 energy, OR deal 3 damage using the energy immediately,
      OR accumulate extra energy and spend more for a bigger kill later.

EXPERIENCE COUNTERS:
  From Commander 2016: specific legendary commanders grant experience counters
  Each experience counter modifies the commander's effectiveness
  Cards like Ezuri, Claw of Progress and Meren of Clan Nel Toth track experience counters on the player
  Experience counters persist: survive commander zone changes, recast, etc.

  EXPERIENCE NOTABLE CARDS:
    Meren of Clan Nel Toth ({2}{B}{G}): "Whenever a creature you control dies, you get an experience
      counter. At the beginning of your end step, choose a creature card in your GY with mana value ≤
      your experience counter count. Return it to the battlefield."
      Accumulate experience: each creature death = +1 experience.
      At 5 experience: can return any creature with MV ≤ 5 each end step.
      At 8+ experience: return almost anything — free recursion engine.

    Ezuri, Claw of Progress ({2}{G}{U}): "Whenever a creature with power 2 or less enters under your
      control, you get an experience counter. At the beginning of your combat, put X +1/+1 counters
      on target creature you control where X = your experience counter count."
      Each small creature entering = +1 experience. Use accumulated experience to pump one big creature.

TICKET COUNTERS (Unfinity):
  Used with Attraction cards
  Gaining tickets: Attractions can say "you get [N] tickets" when visited
  Spending tickets: some Attraction abilities require "have X or more tickets"
  Tickets are a fun, silver-bordered (Acorn) mechanic for the carnival theme

PLAYER COUNTER PERSISTENCE:
  Player counters persist through turn cycles
  Lost only if specifically removed or the player loses the game
  Proliferate adds ONE counter of EACH TYPE a player has:
    Player with 3 poison and 2 energy: proliferate → 4 poison + 3 energy
  Mass effects that "reset counters" rarely exist (Leeches is an old card for poison)
```

## Definitive Conclusions

- **Poison counters at 10 or more = lose the game** — poison is permanent and doesn't reduce on its own.
- **Energy counters are a spendable resource** — generated by effects, spent as activation costs.
- **Experience counters scale commander effectiveness** — persist through all zone changes.
- **Proliferate adds one of each counter type to chosen players AND permanents** — effective for all four types.
- **Player counters are tracked outside of any zone** — they follow the player, not permanents.

## Canonical Example
**Aetherworks Marvel Energy Combo:**
Board: Aetherworks Marvel. Kaladesh aggressive deck.
Turn 1-3: cast several small creatures. They get Wrathed by opponent's sweeper.
Each creature dying: your Marvel triggers "whenever a permanent you control is put into a GY, get {E}."
4 creatures died: you have {E}{E}{E}{E}. Need 6.
Turn 4: sacrifice two Servos for extra mana. Two more GY events → {E}{E}{E}{E}{E}{E}. SIX energy!
Tap Marvel and pay {E}{E}{E}{E}{E}{E}: look at top 6 cards of library.
Find Emrakul, the Promised End (MV 13). Cast it for free. You get Emrakul.
Turn 5: attack with Emrakul. Take control of opponent's turn (Emrakul's Mind Slaver effect).
Aetherworks Marvel: the energy engine that enabled a free 13-mana monster from the top 6.

**Example 2 — Meren Experience Accumulation:**
Commander: Meren of Clan Nel Toth (2 experience counters from prior turns).
Current turn: three creatures die (sacrificed for various effects). +3 experience counters.
Now 5 experience counters.
Beginning of end step: "Return target creature with MV ≤ 5 from GY to battlefield."
5 experience = can return any creature MV ≤ 5. Return Solemn Simulacrum (MV 4) back to battlefield.
Solemn enters: ETB → fetch a land.
Next turn: two more creatures die → 7 experience.
End step: return Grave Titan (MV 6) from GY? No — 6 > 5... still need 6 experience.
Turn after: 8 experience. Return Grave Titan (MV 6) for free each end step.
Experience accumulates permanently — by late game, Meren returns anything for free.
